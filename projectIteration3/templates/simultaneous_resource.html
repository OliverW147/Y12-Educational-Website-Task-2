Maths - Simultaneous Equations

Simultaneous equations are sets of equations with multiple variables that are solved together. The solutions satisfy all equations simultaneously.

Key Concepts in Simultaneous Equations

Below are some foundational concepts in solving simultaneous equations, with explanations, examples, and interactive questions:

1. Solving by Substitution

In this method, you solve one equation for one variable and substitute that expression into the other equation.

Example: Solve the system:

  • Equation 1: y = 2x + 3
  • Equation 2: 3x + y = 9

Solution:

  1. Substitute y in Equation 2: 3x + (2x + 3) = 9
  2. Solve for x: 5x + 3 = 9 ⟹ 5x = 6 ⟹ x = 1.2
  3. Now substitute x back to find y: y = 2(1.2) + 3 = 5.4

Question: Solve the following:

  • Equation 1: y = 3x + 1
  • Equation 2: 2x + y = 11

2. Solving by Elimination

This method involves adding or subtracting the equations to eliminate one variable.

Example: Solve the system:

  • Equation 1: 2x + 3y = 12
  • Equation 2: x - y = 1

Solution:

  1. Rearranging Equation 2 gives x = y + 1.
  2. Substitute into Equation 1: 2(y + 1) + 3y = 12
  3. Solve for y: 2y + 2 + 3y = 12 ⟹ 5y = 10 ⟹ y = 2
  4. Substituting back gives x = 3.

Question: Solve the following:

  • Equation 1: 3x + 4y = 30
  • Equation 2: 2x - y = 9

3. Graphical Method

Graphing both equations on the same axes can help visualize the solution as the point where the lines intersect.

Example: Graph the equations:

  • Equation 1: y = x + 1
  • Equation 2: y = -2x + 4

The intersection point is the solution.

4. Word Problems Involving Simultaneous Equations

Word problems often require setting up equations based on the information given.

Example: A car and a bike together travel 300 km. The car travels 20 km more than twice the distance of the bike. How far does each travel?

Solution:

  • Let b be the distance traveled by the bike, then c = 2b + 20.
  • The equation: b + c = 300 leads to b + (2b + 20) = 300.
  • Solve for b and then find c.
